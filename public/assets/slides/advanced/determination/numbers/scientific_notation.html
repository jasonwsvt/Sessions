Numbers - Scientific Notation:

Very large and very small numbers are often easier to display in scientific notation.

Scientific notation uses the following format: M x 10P, where M is the mantissa, and P is the power.

  • Mantissa: An adjusted value of the actual number, where the decimal point is moved just after the first non-zero digit.
    Range: Real numbers between 0 and 10.
  • Power: The number of places the decimal point must move so that it's just after the first non-zero digit.

Using this way of writing numbers, any number will be multiplied or divided by 10 P times so that M is always between 0 and 10 (as an open range where 0 or 10 are not included).
