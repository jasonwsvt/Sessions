Numbers - Nonstandard Powers:

So now we know how to determine numbers that can reasonably be expressed by a number multiplied by ten to the power of some relatively small integer. What about even larger numbers? Scientific notation utilizes a single expression of power. To express even larger numbers, where the number used as the power is exceptionally high, a second power could be used. A set of up to six powers might be necessary to express a number sufficiently.

The general format is BPQRSTU

This is read B to the P to the Q to the R to the S to the T to the U, where B is usually 10.

Here's the general process I use:

  1. Determine whether 10 is sufficient to be used for A, or if another integer should be used.
  2. Determine the number of powers.
  3. Determine the integer for each power.
