Numbers - Very Small and Large Numbers:

Previously we learned how to determine a number between one and a million or so.

For especially large numbers, or particularly small numbers between 0 and 1, I use scientific notation.

  • Mantissa: An adjusted value of the actual number, where the decimal point is moved just after the first non-zero digit.
         Range: Real numbers between 0 and 10.
  • Precision: The number of digits in the mantissa that are necessary to determine.
         Range: Integers, usually between 0 and 12.
  • Power: The number of places the decimal point must move so that it's just after the first non-zero digit.
         Range: Integers, usually between -1000 and 1000.

Here's the general process I use:

  1. Determine each digit of the power
  2. Determine the precision
  3. Determine each digit of the mantissa, to the required precision.
