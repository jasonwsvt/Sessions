Issue Determination - Intention / Attraction System

My understanding of this system is not complete. My understanding of it has grown as I have continued to clear issues with portions of it as components. As my understanding of it has grown, I have adjusted the system to fit the available data. It might be beneficial to move between this slide and the next while reading the following points.

Here are some observations I've made about this system:

  • The system is an eight tier by fourteen level grid to which an individual's chakras attach.
  • Each tier has a minimally acceptable consciousness level that is acceptable for intention.
    As the tier increases, the minimally acceptable CL decreases.
  • Each level is associated with one chakra, for which an individual has fourteen.
  • Levels 1 - 7 are associated with an individual's self, and correlate to the seven chakras with which we are familiar.
    As an individual controls their intention, they control the placement of their lower seven chakras.
  • levels 8 - 14 and their associated chakras are associated with an individual's experience.
    The individual's subconscious controls the placement of the higher seven chakras.
    My understanding is that their placement is based upon desired learning.
  • Each chakra, and the one or two nodes to which it is attached, are attracted to each other in a way that shapes all of an individual's experiences.
  • My understaning as to why tiers 4 and 5 have the same minimally accepted intention consciousness level is that tier 4 pertains to thoughts, and tier 5 pertains to actions.

There are three rules of this system of which I'm aware:

  • Chakras can only attach to one node, or two nodes on adjacent tiers.
  • Adjacent chakras must have at least one attached tier in common.
  • A new attraction can be strong enough to break previously set attachments, and will do so if necessary to maintain the previous two rules.
