Numbers - Determining the number of places in a smaller number:

When the range of a number is unknown, find the two entries in this list that are closest.

one, ten, hundred, thousand, million, billion, trillion, etc.

Here's the general process I use:

1) Determine the highest amount that's below the number being sought. This number will serve as the minimum of the range. The following entry is just above the maximum.

2) Determine the number of places necessary to be determined.

3) Use binary search to find the number.

To determine the number 23, I would first determine the range - 100, and then I'd use the binary search method - below 50, below 25, above 12, above 18, above 22, 23.

To determine the number 52,340,000, I would first determine the range - million to billion. Then I would then determine whether it's 1, 10, or 100 million, and find that it's 100 million.
