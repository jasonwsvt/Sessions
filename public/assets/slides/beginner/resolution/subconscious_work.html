Issue Resolution - Subconscious Work:

After the resolution command is given, the subconscious then works to resolve the issue.

The internal conflict for the components of the issue are reduced as much as possible, up to and including being cleared from the mind and body.

The point to which each component's internal conflict is reduced is based on cognitive dissonance with remaining work on the given issue, including identification and attachment, along with associated issues.

Cognitive dissonance is a psychologal term describing the tension one experiences between how they feel about the issue, and other associated beliefs and feelings they identify with.

The way the subconscious resolves an issue could be described by elevation. Imagine a helium balloon rising in a room with a ceiling. The height of the ceiling in this room represents the highest point the issue can be resolved and not be cognitively dissonant.
