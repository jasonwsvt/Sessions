Issue Determination - Patterns:

Patterns are learned through life and block the ability to resolve an issueâ€™s reasons, or possibly even determine them.

PatternDescription
ThoughtAn idea, memory, image, or feeling.
BeliefAn opinion one has for something being right or wrong.
BehaviorA way someone conducts themself.
IdentificationThis occurs when someone does not see any separation between themself and something, like a belief or behavior, possibly to the point of being blind to its existance.
AttachmentAn emotional bond one may have with something, like a belief or behavior. One may identify with their reason for the attachment, and not the attachment itself.
ResistanceThe reluctance one might have in accepting or recognizing they identify with or are attached to an issue.

Many patterns often appear in groups for which I have provided the term "pattern stacks" (e.g. the identification with an attachment to a behavior that's based on a belief).
