Psychological Reversals - Overview:

Traditionally, psychological reversals are excuses as to why one should keep an issue. Here is a list of sixteen psychological reversals and example diagnostic statements.

This list is based on one that I was given by my psychotherapist in 2009. Each time I have added a psychological reversal, it has been when one needed to be resolved, but it was not on the list. Sometimes I was able to figure out what it was, and other times it was sufficient to approximate it with a combination of other psychological reversals.

BeliefA specific belief somehow impacts my ability to get over this problem.
BenefitGetting over this problem will be good for me.
CapabilityI'm incapable of getting over this problem.
ContractA contract I must follow will not allow me to get over this problem.
DeservednessI deserve to get over this problem.
DesireI want to get over this problem.
DeprivationI will be (feel) deprived if I get over this problem.
DeterminationI will get over this problem.
DoubtI doubt I will ever get over this problem.
IdentityI will lose my identity if I get over this problem.
MotivationI will do what is necessary to be over this problem.
PermissionI will allow myself to get over this problem.
PossibilityIt is possible for me to get over this problem.
PrivacyGetting over this problem will cause me to lose my privacy.
SafetyIt is safe for me to be over this problem.
ShameMy getting over this problem will cause me to feel ashamed.

Many psychological reversals often appear in groups (e.g. the identification with an attachment to a behavior that's based on a belief).
