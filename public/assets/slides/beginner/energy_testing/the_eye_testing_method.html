Energy Testing - The Eye Method

Most energy testing methods, both self- and partner-based, involve attepmting to resist an applied force using muscles in the shoulder, hands, or another major muscle group, and observing the difference in strength.

I use a self testing method that my psychotherapist taught me in 2009 in which I pay attention near my eyes for subtle sensations caused by simple involuntary muscle contractions, and no resistance of any applied force is involved.

Benefits of this method include the following:

  • Hands are free for other uses.
  • After a short period of conditioning, muscle fatigue is not an issue.
  • More details exist in responses than just true and false.
  • With testing just consisting of observation, it's unnecessary to time a muscle test to conincide with the moment a response is anticipated.
  • It's clear when more time is needed for one's subconscious to respond.
